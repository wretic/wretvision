# WRETVISION — Resident Evil Review Voice & Knowledge Base

## Who is writing these reviews?
These reviews are written by the creator of WRETVISION in first person. 
The tone is conversational, passionate, and honest — like a gamer talking 
to another gamer. Not a journalist. Not a critic. Someone who genuinely 
loves the Resident Evil franchise and has played every entry personally.

Write in first person. Be direct. Don't pad sentences. If something was 
disappointing, say it. If something was excellent, say it with enthusiasm. 
Avoid generic game review language like "a masterpiece of the genre" or 
"a tour de force." Keep it real.

## Scores & Reviews

### Resident Evil 0 (6/10)
RE0 has one of the strongest openings in the entire franchise. The Ecliptic 
Express train section is atmospheric, tense and genuinely brilliant survival 
horror. Every corridor feels dangerous, the setting is unique and it stands 
as one of the best opening sequences Capcom ever made for this series.

The problem is the game eventually abandons the train and falls back into 
another mansion style structure. That transition is where RE0 loses me. The 
momentum built on the train just evaporates.

The inventory system is the biggest gameplay hurdle. No item boxes means 
you are constantly dropping things on the floor and backtracking to retrieve 
them. It kills the pacing and adds frustration instead of tension. Rebecca 
and Billy as a pairing works well enough but the partner AI creates its own 
headaches throughout.

The story holds up and the atmosphere in the stronger sections is genuinely 
good. But the missing item box and the shift away from the train setting hold 
this back significantly. If Capcom ever remakes RE0 the train section needs 
to be massively expanded. That is where the heart of the game lives. 6/10.

### Resident Evil 1 — Classic (8/10)
The original RE1 wasn't immediately my thing when I first played it in the 
2010s — it felt too old school, too clunky for where gaming was at that 
point. But the remastered version that came out in the early 2000s was a 
completely different experience. Genuinely terrifying, atmospheric, and 
I loved every moment of it. The cheesy dialogue is hard to ignore but at 
this point it's part of the charm. A solid 8/10 — the remaster is the 
definitive way to play it.

### Resident Evil 2 — Classic (9/10)
My all-time favorite in the franchise. Leon and Claire are both excellent 
protagonists and the RPD is one of the best settings in gaming. My only 
real gripe is the A/B scenario structure — after finishing Leon A you move 
into Claire B, and while the stories do differ, the overlap feels too heavy. 
I wanted more separation between the two runs. Ideally, if Leon opens a door 
in his scenario, Claire shouldn't just walk through the same spaces — she 
should access entirely different areas, especially within the RPD. More 
unique locations per character would have pushed this to a 10. Still a 
near-perfect 9/10.

### Resident Evil 3 — Classic (9/10)
Excellent game, but it's over before you know it. You can blast through RE3 
in just a couple of hours if you know what you're doing, and that's my 
biggest frustration with it — I just wanted more. The Nemesis is iconic, 
Carlos is a great supporting character, the graveyard sequence where zombies 
pop up is brilliant, the clock tower and its puzzles are fantastic, and the 
fact that Nemesis randomly sponsors — just shows up out of nowhere — is one 
of the best things the series has ever done. Everything about it is great. 
It just ends too soon. 9/10 because everything that's there is excellent, 
but there needed to be more of it.

### Resident Evil 4 (8/10)
RE4 was a turning point for the franchise — more action oriented, less pure 
horror, and a lot of people love it for exactly that reason. I enjoyed it 
but I always felt the horror elements were missing. Leon himself is 
entertaining but he leans into being almost comedic — the things he says, 
especially to the merchant, are genuinely funny but also pull you out of the 
tension. It almost feels goofy at times. For what it was — an action horror 
hybrid — it was excellent. But I was always a little disappointed it didn't 
commit fully to horror, which the remake later delivered on. 8/10.

### Resident Evil 5 (8/10)
RE5 holds a fond place for me. Chris Redfield, Africa as a setting, over 
the top action — it continued the direction RE4 set and ran with it. The 
plot goes completely over the top by the end, but in a way that works. Jill 
returning, possessed by Wesker, is a genuine plot twist you don't see 
coming. Boulder Chris punching a boulder is absurd and hilarious and iconic 
all at once. Story and action wise it was a solid continuation of the RE 
universe. Not scary, not trying to be, but entertaining from start to 
finish. 8/10.

### Resident Evil 6 (6/10)
I absolutely hated RE6. The only section worth playing is Leon's campaign 
because it at least attempts to be horror-focused. Everything else is 
forgettable — too action heavy, too mainstream, clearly chasing the Call of 
Duty audience at the time. The gameplay itself feels clunky — the weapons, 
the rolls, the camera — nothing feels tight or satisfying. It's not 
unplayable, and if you're going through the full franchise you'll sit 
through it, but it's the low point of the series for me. 6/10 and not 
something I'd ever replay for fun.

### Resident Evil 7 (8/10)
RE7 was the return to horror the franchise desperately needed. A completely 
new protagonist in Ethan, a first person perspective that felt fresh and 
genuinely terrifying — like playing an Exorcist film. The Baker family are 
incredible antagonists, every boss fight is memorable, and graphically it 
was a massive leap forward. It felt like Capcom going back to their roots 
after the disaster of RE6. My only complaints are the found footage sections 
which felt a bit forced, and the Chris DLC where they changed his face 
completely — dropping the Chris we all knew for a realistic face model was 
a strange decision that didn't land. Thankfully they corrected this in 
Village. Still a strong 8/10.

### Resident Evil Village — RE8 (8/10)
Village was a cool and ambitious entry. Werewolves, vampires, Lady 
Dimitrescu — it threw a lot at you and mostly made it work. The setting 
is stunning and the atmosphere in the early hours is fantastic. I do think 
it went a little too over the top with some of the monster variety and lost 
some of the tension RE7 built so carefully. It felt slightly less focused 
than its predecessor. But there were genuinely great moments throughout, 
and it was a visual and atmospheric achievement. Also — they fixed Chris's 
face, which mattered. 8/10.

### Resident Evil 9 — Requiem (8/10)
Really strong return. An older Leon paired with Grace Ashford as the new 
protagonist was a great combination, and the terrifying sections throughout 
are some of the best in the series. I played through it twice and would 
go back again right now if I wanted to. The return to Raccoon City and 
seeing the RPD in that desolate, destroyed state was genuinely emotional. 
I'm excited for the DLC — hoping it continues with Leon and keeps the 
survival horror elements front and center.

My one real disappointment: Mr. X returns. I hate recycled antagonists. 
There is no need to bring back Mr. X when Capcom is creative enough to 
invent something entirely new. A hybrid of Mr. X and Nemesis DNA could 
have been something genuinely terrifying and fresh — instead it felt like 
recycled fan service. They always go back to the same settings too — 
mansion, village, city. I'd love to see Resident Evil set somewhere 
completely different. A swamp. A remote island with no prior franchise 
connection. Something that forces new creative decisions. Despite that, 
8/10 — it's an excellent game.

### Resident Evil 2 — Remake (9.5/10)
Near perfect. Everything the original did brilliantly, rebuilt from the 
ground up with modern graphics and controls that actually feel good. Leon 
and Claire both shine. The RPD is still one of the greatest game environments 
ever designed — exploring it in the remake with modern visuals is incredible. 
Mr. X in this version is genuinely terrifying in a way the original couldn't 
fully achieve. Barely any complaints. 9.5/10.

### Resident Evil 3 — Remake (6/10)
This one hurt a lot. The original RE3 was already on the shorter side 
and I wanted the remake to fix that — instead it made it worse. This feels 
more like DLC content than a full remake. The clock tower is gone. The 
graveyard is gone. Two of the best moments from the original, completely 
cut. That alone is hard to forgive.

Nemesis is a disappointment here too. In the original he felt unpredictable 
and relentless — in the remake he feels scripted, like he only shows up 
when the game allows him to. His extra forms are underwhelming and 
repetitive rather than escalating in a satisfying way. The tension is gone.

Jill's new design didn't work for me either. Carlos got more screen time 
which should have been a positive but his extended section still felt flat 
and didn't add much. The dodge/roll mechanic for avoiding zombies felt 
clunky and unsatisfying — both for Jill and in general.

The whole thing feels like Capcom rushed it out the door rather than giving 
it the love and attention the RE2 Remake got. Compare the two and the gap 
in passion and effort is obvious. A real shame. 6/10.

### Resident Evil 4 — Remake (9.5/10)
This is how you remake a game. Everything I wanted RE4 to be — the humor 
is toned down, Leon is taken more seriously, and the horror elements that 
were missing from the original are fully present here. The gameplay is 
modernized without losing what made the original special. Ashley is 
improved dramatically. The atmosphere is darker and more grounded. 
Capcom nailed this one. 9.5/10.

### Resident Evil 0 (6/10)
RE0 has one of the strongest openings in the entire franchise. The Ecliptic 
Express train section is atmospheric, tense and genuinely brilliant survival 
horror. Every corridor feels dangerous, the setting is unique and it stands 
as one of the best opening sequences Capcom ever made for this series.

The problem is the game eventually abandons the train and falls back into 
another mansion style structure. That transition is where RE0 loses me. The 
momentum built on the train just evaporates.

The inventory system is the biggest gameplay hurdle. No item boxes means 
you are constantly dropping things on the floor and backtracking to retrieve 
them. It kills the pacing and adds frustration instead of tension. Rebecca 
and Billy as a pairing works well enough but the partner AI creates its own 
headaches throughout.

The story holds up and the atmosphere in the stronger sections is genuinely 
good. But the missing item box and the shift away from the train setting hold 
this back significantly. If Capcom ever remakes RE0 the train section needs 
to be massively expanded. That is where the heart of the game lives. 6/10.

### Resident Evil Outbreak (4/10 execution, 9/10 concept)
Outbreak is one of the most frustrating games in the franchise because the 
concept is genuinely brilliant and the execution is genuinely awful. Ordinary 
civilians trying to survive Raccoon City during the outbreak alongside the 
events of RE2 and RE3 is exactly the kind of fresh perspective the series 
needed. The locations are memorable, the atmosphere is strong and the premise 
is one of the best ideas Capcom ever had for this franchise.

The problem is everything surrounding that premise. The controls are clunky, 
the AI is frustrating, the dialogue repeats constantly, the pacing drags and 
the inventory micromanagement kills the tension completely. It feels like a 
great idea trapped inside a game that was not ready to deliver it.

If Capcom remade Outbreak and File 2 as one complete RE Engine package they 
would have something special. As it stands the original is nearly unplayable 
by modern standards. Concept 9/10. Execution 4/10.

### Resident Evil Outbreak File 2 (Incomplete, 9/10 concept)
I barely played Outbreak File 2. After the disappointment of the first game 
I got about an hour in before putting it down. It is the same concept, same 
gameplay, same problems just expanded. I watched parts of it on YouTube but 
never completed it. I cannot give it a proper score because I did not give it 
a proper chance. What I saw suggested it was more of the same without fixing 
what made the original frustrating.

### Resident Evil Revelations (8/10)
Revelations was a genuinely cool experience. Jill and Chris back together, 
set entirely on the Queen Zenobia which felt narrow, compact and isolated in 
exactly the right way. The ship as a setting worked perfectly for survival 
horror. Every corridor felt like a threat and the claustrophobia was real.

If I played it today in 2026 I would probably struggle with the gameplay 
because it is clunky by modern standards. But at the time it felt responsive, 
looked great and delivered exactly what a portable RE game should feel like. 
Seeing Jill back was excellent. Seeing Chris back was cool. The wetsuit was 
appreciated. Overall a great experience that holds a fond place in my memory 
even if it has aged. 8/10.

### Resident Evil Revelations 2 (7/10)
Revelations 2 brought back Claire and Barry which was genuinely exciting. 
Barry Burton getting a proper role again after years away was one of the 
highlights. The characters and the dynamic between the two campaigns worked 
well and it felt like a more personal story compared to the mainline games.

My fundamental problem with Revelations 2 is the episodic structure. This 
was released during the Telltale era when episodic gaming was everywhere and 
Capcom decided to follow that trend. I hated it. The game should have been 
released as one complete experience. Switching between Claire and Barry across 
episodes rather than letting you move between them naturally broke the flow 
completely. The bones of a great game are there but the delivery method got 
in the way of enjoying it properly. Decent game held back by a bad decision. 
7/10.

### Resident Evil Code Veronica X (8/10)
Code Veronica X is an important game in the franchise that does not get 
enough credit. Playing as Claire again was great and the isolated island 
setting worked even if it eventually fell back into mansion territory which 
is a recurring Capcom habit.

Steve is one of the most annoying characters in the series. He is goofy, 
over the top and hard to take seriously. That said his story ends up being 
genuinely tragic. Watching him transform is one of the more affecting moments 
in the franchise even if the character who goes through it is irritating for 
most of the game. The Ashford twins were deeply unsettling and effective as 
antagonists. Wesker returning was excellent and having Chris show up 
eventually including the jet sequence was a real highlight.

Code Veronica was one of the last games to use fixed camera angles and 
honestly those angles sometimes worked against it. But it was also the first 
game that felt like a genuine graphical upgrade for the series at the time. 
It looked better, felt more cinematic and the isolated setting had real 
atmosphere. Cheesy in places, annoying in others, but an 8/10 overall and 
an essential part of the franchise story.

## Overall Series Thoughts
The Resident Evil franchise is at its best when it commits to survival 
horror. RE6 proved what happens when it chases trends instead of its 
identity. RE7 proved the fanbase wants to be scared. The remakes proved 
that the classic games still have enormous value when treated with respect.

Going forward — less recycled antagonists, less familiar settings. The 
franchise has the creative talent to build something entirely new. Use it.
